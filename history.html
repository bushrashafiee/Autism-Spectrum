THE TIMELINE AND HISTORY OF AUTISM

1747 - Well documented first case of autism, where Hugh Blair of
Borgue's broter petitioned to annul
Blair's marrying to his wife to get to Blair's inheritance

(Court documents describing Blair show very stromgly that he had autistic symptoms)

1798 - The Wild Boy of Aveyron, who was a feral child
who lived in wild for seven years
Researchers to believe that the boy had Autism
based on his ability to learn to communicate and some of his behaviors.

Early 1900 - Bleuler coined the term Autism

Before 1938 - Autism started to take on a more modern meaning
and separation from schizophrenia.
Hans Asperger of the Vienna University Hospitals research in investigating
what came to be known as Asperger syndrome

1943 - Autism became even more defined when Leo Kanner came up with early infantile
autism to describe the behavioral similarities in 11 children he was working with

(1943 - 1960) - Kanner’s diagnosis there was a lot of confusion around the term Autism,
with it getting mixed up with terminology like infantile schizophrenia.

Late 60's to mid 70's - autism started to get more attention, research, and definition

1964 - Bernard Rimland published "Infantile Autism: The Syndrome and it's Implication for Natural Theory of Behavior"

(60's-70's) - Consisted of LSD, electric shock, and behaviour change
that focused on pain was used as treatments.

1987 - “infantile autism” was expanded into “autism disorder” and finally
had a set list of diagnostic criteria to help standardize the diagnosing
Ivar Lovaas published the first study showing that intensive behavior
therapy can help children improve their autistic symptoms.

1991 - U.S. Government made autism a special education category, which helped public schools to offer
special services to children on the spectrum.

1980’s and 1990’s - Not all positive movements

1998 - a study was published that the measles-mumps-rubella (MMR) vaccine causes autism.
